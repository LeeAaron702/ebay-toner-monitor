Overhead Percentage

This percentage is deducted from the Amazon sale price to account for hidden costs: inbound shipping, return allowances, packaging/prep, and safety margin.

How it works

Formula: Effective Net = Seller Proceeds - (Amazon Price × {{ overhead_pct }}%)

Example: If Amazon price is $50 and Seller Proceeds is $30, with {{ overhead_pct }}% overhead:
Effective Net = $30 - ($50 × {{ overhead_pct / 100 }}) = $30 - ${{ "%.2f"|format(50 * overhead_pct / 100) }} = ${{ "%.2f"|format(30 - 50 * overhead_pct / 100) }}

What the overhead covers

  • Inbound shipping: $0.50-2.00+ per unit to Amazon warehouse
  • Return allowances: ~2-5% of units become unsellable returns
  • Packaging/prep: Labels, poly bags, boxes (~$0.20-0.50/unit)
  • Cost to carry: Storage fees + capital tied up
  • Safety margin: Buffer for price fluctuations
